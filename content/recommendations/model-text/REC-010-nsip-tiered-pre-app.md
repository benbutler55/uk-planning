# REC-010: Tiered NSIP Pre-Application Process

## Problem

The Planning Act 2008 pre-application process (sections 42-47) requires all NSIP applicants to undertake the same extensive public and statutory consultation regardless of scheme scale or controversy. A 100MW solar farm in a non-sensitive location faces the same procedural requirements as a 3GW offshore wind project. Pre-application durations of 18-24 months are standard, adding significant cost and delay before examination can commence, deterring investment in nationally important infrastructure.

## Proposed Change

Amend the Planning Act 2008 to introduce a three-tier NSIP pre-application process based on scheme scale, environmental sensitivity, and community impact, with proportionate consultation requirements for each tier.

## Model Primary Legislation Text

> **Planning Act 2008 — proposed amendment to section 42**
>
> 42A— Tiered pre-application consultation
>
> (1) The Secretary of State shall by regulations designate three tiers of NSIP pre-application consultation:
>
> **Tier 1 — Standard track:** Applicable to schemes below [threshold] MW or [threshold] km length, in non-designated areas with no significant transboundary impacts. Pre-application consultation period: minimum 6 months.
>
> **Tier 2 — Enhanced track:** Applicable to schemes above Tier 1 thresholds but below NSIP upper thresholds, or in nationally designated landscapes. Pre-application consultation period: minimum 12 months.
>
> **Tier 3 — Full track:** Applicable to the largest or most complex NSIP schemes. Pre-application consultation period: minimum 18 months.
>
> (2) The Planning Inspectorate shall publish track allocation guidance and shall confirm track designation within 4 weeks of a developer submitting a Track Allocation Request.
>
> (3) A developer allocated to Tier 1 is not required to comply with the section 47 Statement of Community Consultation requirements applicable to Tier 2 and Tier 3 schemes.

## Implementation Notes

- Requires primary legislation amendment to PA 2008.
- Regulations under 42A to be laid by statutory instrument.
- PINS to update Advice Note 1 (pre-application prospectus guidance) to reflect tiered approach.
- Pilot Tier 1 track informally for 6 months before legislation using PINS discretionary guidance.

## KPI

- Target: median pre-application duration for Tier 1 schemes reduced from 24 months to under 12 months.
- Baseline: median NSIP pre-application period 18-24 months (PINS quarterly statistics).
