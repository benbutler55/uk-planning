# REC-008: Binding Consultee Response Deadline

## Problem

The DMPO 2015 sets a 21-day consultation period but imposes no consequence on statutory consultees who fail to respond within it. In practice, non-responses routinely extend consultation windows, delay committee reports, and increase officer holding time. Highway authorities and the Environment Agency are the most frequent sources of late or absent responses.

## Proposed Change

Amend DMPO 2015 to introduce a binding 21-day response deadline for statutory consultees on non-complex applications. Failure to respond within 21 days triggers a deemed no-objection for the purposes of that consultee's statutory function, unless the application has been flagged as complex at the pre-application stage.

## Model SI Amendment Text

> **Town and Country Planning (Development Management Procedure) (England) (Amendment) Order [Year]**
>
> Amendment to Article 17 (consultation before the grant of permission):
>
> (1A) Where a statutory consultee listed in Schedule 4 has been consulted in accordance with this Article and has not provided a response within 21 days beginning with the date the consultation was sent, the local planning authority may proceed to determine the application without further delay and shall record in the decision notice that the consultee provided no response within the statutory period.
>
> (1B) A statutory consultee who has not responded within 21 days shall be treated as having raised no objection for the purposes of that consultee's statutory function, except where—
> (a) the application has been designated Complex at the pre-application stage by agreement between the applicant, the LPA, and the relevant consultee; or
> (b) the application is an EIA application subject to Schedule 4 consultation requirements.
>
> (1C) A deemed no-objection under paragraph (1B) shall not prevent a statutory consultee from raising concerns at appeal, but the Planning Inspector shall have regard to whether those concerns could reasonably have been raised within the statutory consultation period.

## Implementation Notes

- Requires DMPO 2015 amendment — SI, no primary legislation.
- Complex application designation to be agreed at pre-application meeting.
- MHCLG to publish updated guidance on statutory consultee obligations.

## KPI

- Target: 90 percent of consultees responding within 21 days on non-complex applications.
- Baseline: estimated 60-70 percent response rate within statutory period (PINS ministerial measures proxy).
