# REC-009: Viability Assessment Lock-In at Plan Adoption

## Problem

PPG Viability permits viability to be re-assessed at application stage even where the development plan was informed by viability testing at plan preparation stage. This creates systematic uncertainty for allocated sites: developers cannot rely on plan-stage viability assessments, and LPAs cannot enforce plan-stage affordable housing requirements without risk of viability challenge at appeal. The result is chronic under-delivery of affordable housing and protracted negotiation.

## Proposed Change

Amend PPG Viability to establish that viability assessments at plan-making stage are binding for allocated sites, and that application-stage viability re-assessment is only permissible where the developer can demonstrate a material change in market conditions since plan adoption using a prescribed methodology.

## Model PPG Amendment Text

> **PPG Viability — proposed paragraph [X]**
>
> For allocated sites where the development plan was informed by a site-specific or area-wide viability assessment at plan preparation stage, that assessment shall be treated as the starting point for any application-stage viability consideration. A developer seeking to depart from plan-stage obligations must demonstrate, using the prescribed viability review methodology, that:
>
> (a) build costs or land values have changed by more than 15 percent since the plan-stage assessment was completed; and
> (b) the development would not be economically viable at the policy-compliant level of affordable housing without the reduction sought.
>
> The prescribed viability review methodology shall be the standard appraisal model published by [MHCLG/RICS], using benchmark land values set at plan stage unless revised by the LPA within the preceding 2 years.
>
> Where a viability review is submitted under this paragraph, the LPA shall respond within 8 weeks with a written assessment. The cost of the LPA's independent review shall be met by the applicant.

## Implementation Notes

- No statutory instrument required — PPG amendment by ministerial direction.
- MHCLG to commission standard appraisal model with RICS.
- LPAs to update local validation checklists to flag viability review trigger.

## KPI

- Target: 50 percent reduction in viability re-assessment rate on allocated sites.
- Baseline: estimated 30-40 percent of allocated site applications subject to viability re-assessment (PPG monitoring and practitioner evidence).
