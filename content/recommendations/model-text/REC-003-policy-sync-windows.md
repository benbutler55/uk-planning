# REC-003: Synchronized Annual Policy Update Windows

## Problem

MHCLG updates the NPPF and PPG on a rolling basis with no predictable schedule. Local plans are reviewed on varying cycles. This creates frequent periods where national policy has moved but local plans have not been updated, generating policy conflict that decision-makers must resolve case by case, and which drives appeal uncertainty.

## Proposed Change

MHCLG to establish a fixed annual policy update window (Q4 each year) for substantive NPPF and PPG changes, with a minimum 12-month transition before those changes have full weight in decision-making for authorities with adopted plans less than 5 years old.

## Model Protocol Text

> **National Planning Policy Update Protocol (model)**
>
> 1. Substantive amendments to the NPPF and PPG shall ordinarily be made within a defined annual window of [October to December] each year.
>
> 2. Where an amendment affects the weight to be given to adopted development plan policies, local planning authorities with a plan adopted within the preceding 5 years shall have a 12-month transition period during which the pre-amendment policy shall carry equal weight to the amended policy in decision-making.
>
> 3. MHCLG shall publish a forward programme of planned policy changes no later than [March] each year to enable local plan preparation teams to anticipate changes.
>
> 4. Emergency amendments outside the annual window are permitted where required by primary legislation, judicial decision, or material national security or public safety consideration.

## Implementation Notes

- No statutory vehicle required — can be implemented by MHCLG administrative practice and ministerial statement.
- PPG section on plan-making to be updated to reflect the protocol.
- Local plan examination inspectors to be directed to apply the transition protocol consistently.

## KPI

- Target: 40 percent reduction in policy conflict incidents logged per quarter across pilot LPAs.
- Baseline: over 50 percent of local plans more than 5 years old; rolling NPPF updates logged (NPPF revision history).
