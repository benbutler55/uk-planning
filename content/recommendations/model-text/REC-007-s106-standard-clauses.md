# REC-007: Standard s106 Heads of Terms and Model Clauses

## Problem

Post-resolution negotiation of Section 106 planning obligations is unregulated in duration. Each LPA uses bespoke legal drafting, requiring full re-negotiation for each scheme. Developers, solicitors, and LPA legal teams spend 6-18 months completing agreements that are substantially similar across schemes. This is the second largest post-permission delay driver after condition discharge.

## Proposed Change

MHCLG to publish a statutory library of standard s106 heads of terms and model clauses for the most common obligation types (affordable housing, education, transport, open space, biodiversity net gain). LPAs to be required to use model clauses unless they can demonstrate a specific local circumstance requiring departure, documented in the decision notice.

## Model Standard Clause (Affordable Housing)

> **Standard Section 106 Affordable Housing Obligation — Model Clause**
>
> 1. **Provision obligation:** The Owner shall not Commence Development unless and until the Local Planning Authority has approved in writing an Affordable Housing Scheme in accordance with the following:
>    - Not less than [X]% of Dwellings shall be Affordable Housing Units
>    - Of which not less than [Y]% shall be Social Rent and not less than [Z]% shall be Shared Ownership or First Homes
>
> 2. **Delivery obligation:** Affordable Housing Units shall be provided in accordance with the approved scheme and transferred to an Approved Registered Provider prior to Occupation of [50]% of Market Dwellings.
>
> 3. **Cascade provision:** If no Registered Provider has agreed to take the Affordable Housing Units within [6] months of practical completion of those units, the Owner shall give written notice to the Local Planning Authority and may, with the Authority's written agreement, convert no more than [X]% of Affordable Units to a commuted sum at a rate of £[rate per unit] in lieu of on-site provision.
>
> 4. **Definition — Affordable Housing:** Has the meaning given in the National Planning Policy Framework at [date of permission].

## Implementation Notes

- MHCLG to publish model clause library via updated PPG on planning obligations.
- No statutory instrument required — guidance-led reform.
- Law Society and local government solicitors to be consulted on drafting.
- LPA legal teams to receive training materials within 6 months of publication.

## KPI

- Target: median time from resolution to legal completion reduced from 12 months to under 6 months.
- Baseline: estimated 9-18 month median (MHCLG delivery evidence and practitioner surveys).
