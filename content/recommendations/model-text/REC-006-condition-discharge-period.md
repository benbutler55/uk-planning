# REC-006: Statutory Period for Pre-Commencement Condition Discharge

## Problem

Applications to discharge pre-commencement planning conditions attract no statutory determination period under current rules. LPAs routinely take 3-12 months to discharge conditions, blocking site start and imposing holding costs on developers without any regulatory backstop. This is the single largest post-permission delay driver identified across pilot LPAs.

## Proposed Change

Amend the DMPO 2015 to introduce a statutory 8-week determination period for pre-commencement condition discharge applications, equivalent to the minor application period, with a deemed discharge provision after 12 weeks if the LPA has not issued a decision or requested an extension.

## Model SI Amendment Text

> **Town and Country Planning (Development Management Procedure) (England) (Amendment) Order [Year]**
>
> Amendment to Article [X] (applications to discharge or vary conditions):
>
> (1) An application to discharge a pre-commencement planning condition shall be determined by the local planning authority within a period of 8 weeks beginning with the date the application is validated, unless—
> (a) the applicant and the authority agree in writing to an extended period; or
> (b) the application involves a condition requiring approval of details subject to consultation under Schedule 4 of these Regulations, in which case a 13-week period applies.
>
> (2) Where a local planning authority has not determined an application under paragraph (1) within 12 weeks of validation, and no extension has been agreed, the condition shall be treated as discharged to the extent necessary to permit commencement of development, subject to any material planning objection raised in writing by a statutory consultee within the 8-week period.
>
> (3) A local planning authority that fails to determine more than [20] percent of condition discharge applications within the statutory period in any rolling 12-month period shall be placed on the Planning Performance Framework monitoring list.

## Implementation Notes

- Statutory instrument amendment to DMPO 2015 — no primary legislation required.
- Deemed discharge provision requires careful drafting to exclude safety-critical conditions (contamination, structural stability).
- LPAs to be given 6-month preparation period before new rules take effect.

## KPI

- Target: median condition discharge time under 8 weeks.
- Baseline: no current statutory baseline; estimated 3-12 month median (pilot LPA monitoring).
