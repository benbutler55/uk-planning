# REC-011: Updated EIA Screening Thresholds

## Problem

EIA screening thresholds in Schedule 2 of the Town and Country Planning (Environmental Impact Assessment) Regulations 2017 have not been substantively updated to reflect current development typologies or scales. Many thresholds were set when development patterns were materially different. The result is systematic over-screening of smaller urban schemes (e.g. brownfield residential above 0.5 ha) and under-screening of cumulative impacts from multiple smaller developments. This imposes disproportionate cost on lower-impact schemes and misses genuine cumulative environmental risk.

## Proposed Change

Update Schedule 2 EIA thresholds to reflect current development scales, introduce a mandatory cumulative impact screening gateway for multiple consented schemes in a defined area, and introduce a simplified scoping process for brownfield EIA applications.

## Model SI Amendment Text

> **Town and Country Planning (Environmental Impact Assessment) (Amendment) Regulations [Year]**
>
> Schedule 2 amendment:
>
> Column 1 (Description of development) — Urban development projects:
> Revised threshold: [1 ha] for residential development on previously developed land; [0.5 ha] for residential development on greenfield land (replacing the current uniform 0.5 ha threshold).
>
> New entry — Cumulative impact screening:
> Where three or more development projects within a [500m] radius have been granted planning permission within the preceding [36] months and their combined floor area or site area exceeds [the applicable individual Schedule 2 threshold], a cumulative impact screening opinion shall be required before submission of any further application in the area.
>
> New Article [X] — Simplified brownfield scoping:
> Where development is proposed on land with an existing planning history, the LPA may issue a simplified scoping opinion using the prescribed template within 5 weeks, limiting assessment topics to those where significant effects cannot be excluded on the basis of the existing planning record.

## Implementation Notes

- Requires statutory instrument amendment under European Communities Act 1972 successor powers (Retained EU Law Act 2023 mechanism).
- MHCLG to consult Environment Agency, Historic England, and Natural England on revised thresholds.
- Estimated 18-24 month process from consultation to commencement.

## KPI

- Target: 40 percent reduction in over-screening rate for brownfield residential schemes below 1 ha.
- Baseline: estimated 20-30 percent of Schedule 2 applications currently over-screened (EIA statistics and PINS screening records).
