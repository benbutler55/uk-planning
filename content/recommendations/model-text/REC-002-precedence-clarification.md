# REC-002: Precedence Rules for Strategic Housing Conflicts

## Problem

Where a local plan restricts housing development using criteria that conflict with national policy obligations to meet strategic housing need (NPPF paragraph 11), decision-makers and appellants apply inconsistent interpretations. The current framework relies on case-by-case weighing without clear rules, generating divergent appeal outcomes for comparable schemes.

## Proposed Change

Amend the NPPF and introduce a statutory instrument under section 38(6) PCPA 2004 to establish a clear precedence hierarchy: where a development plan policy conflicts with a strategic housing delivery obligation, the national obligation prevails unless the LPA can demonstrate a specific and substantial harm that outweighs the strategic benefit.

## Model Policy Text (NPPF Amendment)

> **Proposed NPPF paragraph [X] (model)**
>
> Where a development plan policy would prevent delivery of the housing requirement identified in the most recent Local Housing Need assessment, that policy shall be given reduced weight in the decision unless the local planning authority can demonstrate, on the basis of evidence presented at the time of determination, that:
>
> (a) there are specific and substantial harms that demonstrably outweigh the strategic benefit of the housing delivery; and
>
> (b) no reasonable alternative site is available to meet the unmet need without triggering those harms.
>
> For the purposes of this paragraph, general conformity with a development boundary, settlement hierarchy, or design policy alone does not constitute a specific and substantial harm.

## Model SI Text

> **Town and Country Planning (Strategic Housing Precedence) (England) Regulations [Year]**
>
> 3.— (1) In determining an application for planning permission for residential development, a local planning authority or inspector must have regard to whether a development plan policy relied upon to refuse permission is consistent with the authority's identified Local Housing Need.
>
> (2) Where a development plan policy is materially inconsistent with the authority's five-year housing land supply position, that policy shall carry no more than moderate weight unless the conditions in NPPF paragraph [X] are met.

## Implementation Notes

- Primary vehicle: NPPF amendment (Secretary of State direction) plus supporting SI.
- Consultation required before SI laid.
- Updated PPG on decision-taking to reflect new hierarchy.

## KPI

- Target: 20 percent reduction in housing refusal appeal overturn rate.
- Baseline: approximately 2.1 percent of major decisions overturned at appeal (P152).
